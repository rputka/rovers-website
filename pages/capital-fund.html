About the Fund

Cleveland, OH: The Cleveland Rovers Rugby Football Club announced the start of a capital development fund aimed at acquiring and building a permanent athletic facility, with the long-term intention of partnering with The Cleveland Foundation. As a federal 501(c)(3) charity, the mission of Cleveland Rovers R.F.C. Inc. is to foster national and international amateur sports competition, and to support and develop amateur athletes for that competition.

"The Rovers are excited to kick off this campaign by investing $20,000 from the team operating fund as seed money for a bright future. All donations and grants will be used for the acquisition, construction, and operation of a permanent home for the Rovers and the entire Cleveland rugby community," said Dan Rabkewych, President, Cleveland Rovers. "We hope to gain support from the entire Cleveland sports and business communities for this project. The facility will be used by future generations, for all sports, for many years to come."

Fund Goals

  • Assessment and Acquisition of Land
  • Construction of Athletic Fields and Structures
  • Maintenance and Operation of the Facility
  • Future Growth

The Rovers are currently reviewing several potential sites in and around the city, and have partnered with architectural consultant Kenny Levick of Blue Shift Industries on initial concept renderings of the future facility and grounds.

All inquiries regarding the Capital Development Fund, including how to donate, can be sent to ClevelandRovers@gmail.com.
